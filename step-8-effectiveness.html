Phases F + G - Effectiveness + loop-back / Step 8 of 10

Verify effectiveness. Close or loop back.

30/60/90 day checkpoints with evidence. Close the CAPA only when the metric moves. If not, re-enter at the weakest link.

Why this matters
CAPA closure requires evidence of effectiveness. PDCA's Check phase tells you whether the countermeasure worked. If metrics did not move, re-enter: was the root cause wrong, the countermeasure inadequate, or execution incomplete?
Source: LEI PDCA

Loop-back diagnosis (when not effective)

If a checkpoint verdict is not effective, work through these three questions before re-entering:

  1. Was the root cause wrong? Go back to Step 4 and test the evidence.
  2. Was the countermeasure inadequate? Go back to Step 5 and design a stronger action.
  3. Did execution fail? Check governance (Step 6): ownership, follow-through, standardization.
